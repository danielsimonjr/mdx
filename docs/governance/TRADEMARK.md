# MDZ Format — Trademark guidelines (Draft)

**Status:** Draft. Not yet filed with any trademark office. If adoption
milestones hit, the "MDZ Format" wordmark + logo will be filed in
relevant jurisdictions (US, EU, UK, Japan) to protect the name against
confusion with competing formats.

**Purpose:** clarify how third parties may refer to MDZ in their
products, documentation, and marketing — so users can identify
MDZ-compatible tools while protecting the brand from misleading uses.

---

## Trademarks in scope

- **MDZ™** (word mark)
- **MDZ Format™** (word mark)
- **mdz-format.org** domain
- Planned logo (currently absent; will be filed alongside the word
  marks when logo is designed)

The aliases **.mdz** (file extension) and **.mdx** (legacy extension
through 2027-01-01) are not trademark-protected — file extensions are
generally unregistrable.

## Nominative fair use (allowed without permission)

You may use "MDZ" or "MDZ Format" to describe tools, products, and
services that work with MDZ archives, provided:

1. You're **accurately describing** compatibility or interaction. For
   example:
   - ✅ "Supports MDZ archives"
   - ✅ "MDZ Format reader"
   - ✅ "Import from MDZ"
   - ✅ "Compatible with MDZ Format v2.0"
2. You **do not imply endorsement** by the MDZ Format project,
   editors, or community unless you have explicit written endorsement.
3. You **do not imply certification** of conformance unless your tool
   has actually passed the public conformance suite. If it has, you may
   say "passes the MDZ Format conformance suite" with a link to the
   specific run.
4. Your use is **truthful and not misleading**. Marketing a format
   that's merely inspired-by as "MDZ-compatible" is trademark abuse.

## Disallowed uses (require permission)

- **Product names** starting with or prominently featuring "MDZ" as if
  it were yours. Not OK: "MDZ Pro", "MDZ Studio", "MDZ Cloud". OK:
  "Acme Paper Editor" with a "supports MDZ" feature list.
- **Derivative marks** like "MDZ-Plus", "MDZFormat+", "TrueMDZ" — the
  suffixed forms imply relationship.
- **Domain names** containing "mdz" or "mdz-format" as the primary
  mark. Exceptions for clear-cut descriptive use (e.g.,
  `mdz-viewer.company.com` for an MDZ-compatible viewer your company
  runs).
- **Logo usage**, whenever there's a registered logo. Until a logo is
  filed, this restriction is dormant.

## Packaging and attribution

If you ship a tool that reads or writes MDZ, you may (and SHOULD):

1. List MDZ in your feature comparison / format-support matrix.
2. Link to `mdz-format.org` (or its successor) when explaining MDZ
   capabilities.
3. Reproduce the spec version number your tool supports (e.g., "MDZ
   Format v2.1").
4. Include this attribution line if you wish (not required):
   ```
   Supports MDZ Format. MDZ is a project of the MDZ Format Community Group.
   See https://mdz-format.org/ for the specification.
   ```

## Fonts, colors, and style

Until a logo is registered, there's no visual style to protect.
After logo registration, additional guidelines will cover:

- Permitted color palettes.
- Minimum clear-space around the logo.
- Prohibited deformations (stretched, skewed, recolored).
- Black-and-white fallback rules.

## Enforcement

The trademark owner (once filed) will pursue **uses that confuse
users about the source or quality of MDZ-related software**. Typical
enforcement approach:

1. **Polite contact** — the owner emails the infringing party
   explaining the issue and suggesting a remedy.
2. **Public record** — if contact fails, a brief public statement on
   `mdz-format.org` noting the specific infringement.
3. **Formal action** — only as last resort, and only for commercially
   significant infringement that hurts users.

We will **not** pursue:

- Academic papers discussing MDZ (fair use).
- Blog posts, tutorials, and comparison articles.
- Open-source implementations that follow the nominative-fair-use
  rules.
- "Works with MDZ" badges made by the ecosystem, provided they're
  accurate.

## Precedents and inspiration

This document takes cues from:

- **The Python trademark policy** (permissive nominative fair use,
  restrictive derivative marks).
- **The Rust trademark policy** (attempted revision in 2023 taught
  us what NOT to do — don't let aggressive language scare off the
  community).
- **The Linux Foundation Project trademark pattern** (held in trust
  by a neutral party).

Our guiding principle: **the trademark exists to protect users from
confusion, not to extract value from the ecosystem.**
